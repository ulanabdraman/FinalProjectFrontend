About Mercedes-Benz

Mercedes-Benz is not just a car; it is an experience. A German global automobile marque and a division of Daimler AG, Mercedes-Benz has redefined the automotive industry with its combination of luxury, performance, and state-of-the-art engineering. From its origins over a century ago to its position today as a global leader, Mercedes-Benz continues to set the bar for what a luxury vehicle should be.

Founded in 1926, Mercedes-Benz was born from the unification of two pioneers in the automotive world: Karl Benz, inventor of the first practical automobile, and Gottlieb Daimler, whose innovations revolutionized engine technology. The brand quickly became a symbol of excellence, crafting vehicles that merged engineering prowess with the highest standards of craftsmanship.

Milestones in Mercedes-Benz History

  • 1886 - Karl Benz's Benz Patent-Motorwagen, the world's first automobile, is created.
  • 1926 - The brand name "Mercedes-Benz" is officially adopted after the merger of Benz & Cie. and Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft (DMG).
  • 1951 - Mercedes-Benz introduces the first-ever safety feature: the crumple zone.
  • 1989 - The brand launches its first luxury SUV, the Mercedes-Benz M-Class, pioneering a new era of luxury off-road vehicles.
  • 2019 - The Mercedes-Benz EQC, the brand's first fully-electric luxury SUV, marks the beginning of its commitment to sustainable, eco-friendly mobility.
